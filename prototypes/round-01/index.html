About the Lab

PHI Lab φ

PHI (Precision & Provenance Health Informatics Lab) at the Catholic University of Korea is dedicated to advancing precision medicine and digital healthcare through data-driven interdisciplinary research. The lab's work spans from public data to EHR data, emphasizing trustworthy data utilization, knowledge generation via data science, and supporting decision-making through data-based digital healthcare systems.

가톨릭대학교 PHI(φ, Precision & Provenance Health Informatics Lab) 연구실에서는 정밀의료와 디지털 헬스케어 구현을 위한 데이터 기반 다학제연구를 수행합니다. 공공데이터부터 EHR 데이터까지 포괄적으로 연구하며, 신뢰할 수 있는 데이터의 활용, 데이터 과학을 통한 지식 창출, 의사결정을 지원하는 데이터 기반 디지털 헬스케어 시스템 전반을 연구합니다.

"Data is the new soil, not oil."

— David McCandless, Information is Beautiful

Oil is valuable and tradable. Data only becomes valuable with considerable effort. In the data-driven era, the effectiveness of AI machines depends on the granularity and precision of the data. We focus on optimal informative transformation with the right application, so that it could be a medium that bridges the gap between raw data and actionable knowledge.

What We Do

Research Areas

PHI Lab bridges clinical informatics, AI, and pharmacoepidemiology to generate trustworthy evidence from real-world healthcare data.

Knowledge Representation

Data modeling, data engineering, end-to-end pipeline construction, and data management & governance for interoperable healthcare information systems.

Data Modeling Engineering Governance

Real-World Data (RWD)

Secondary use of electronic health records and clinical data warehouse data, plus public repositories including FAERS for large-scale observational research.

EHR / CDW FAERS Secondary Use

Real-World Evidence (RWE)

Scientific data processing and rigorous analytics for systematic evidence generation from observational clinical sources.

Causal Inference Analytics Evidence

Pharmacovigilance

Drug safety monitoring, adverse event signal detection via spontaneous reporting systems including FAERS and HIRA post-market surveillance databases.

Drug Safety Signal Detection HIRA

Healthcare AI

LLM evaluation in clinical settings, clinical named entity recognition (NER), and deep learning phenotyping for patient characterization.

LLM Evaluation Clinical NER Deep Learning

Precision Medicine

Genomic data integration, cancer trajectory analysis, and clinical decision support system (CDSS) development for personalized care.

Genomics Cancer Trajectory CDSS

Principal Investigator

Hyo Jung Kim

Assistant Professor — Dept. of Biomedical Software Engineering, The Catholic University of Korea

Hyo Jung Kim is an associate professor in the Department of Biomedical Software Engineering at The Catholic University of Korea, South Korea. She earned her BSc and MSc in Nursing, and Management & Education from Yonsei University in Seoul, South Korea, and her Ph.D. in Biomedical Informatics from Seoul National University. She is a seasoned clinical informaticist with over 20 years of professional experience across clinical practice, EHR software architecture, and academic research. She focuses on integrative approaches to enhancing continuity and quality of care enabled by the digital landscape from knowledge representation to artificial intelligence system.
