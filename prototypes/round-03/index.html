Precision & Provenance Health Informatics Lab

We bridge data science and implementation science to generate real-world evidence from clinical data. Our work spans knowledge representation, secondary use of EHR and claims data, and AI-driven phenotyping — with a focus on cancer, pharmacovigilance, and health equity.

Research Areas

Knowledge Representation

Data modeling, biomedical ontology, clinical data engineering, pipeline construction, and governance for precision medicine applications. Includes clinical genome data modeling (cGDM) and interoperability frameworks.

Real-World Data (RWD)

Secondary use of electronic health records (EHR), clinical data warehouses (CDW), FAERS, and Korean claims data (HIRA). Registry construction, cohort definition, and data quality management for multi-institutional studies.

Real-World Evidence (RWE)

Scientific data processing for evidence generation and causal inference. Pharmacovigilance signal detection, treatment effectiveness evaluation, and integration of social determinants of health with clinical outcomes.

About the Lab

The Precision & Provenance Health Informatics Lab (PHI Lab, φ) is a research laboratory in the Department of Biomedical Software Engineering at The Catholic University of Korea. The lab is led by Professor Hyo Jung Kim, a clinical informaticist with over two decades of experience in clinical practice, hospital information systems, and academic research.

Our research focuses on three interconnected pillars: knowledge representation of clinical and genomic data, secondary use of real-world data from EHR systems and administrative claims, and the generation of real-world evidence to support precision medicine and public health decision-making. We conduct multi-institutional studies in collaboration with major Korean hospitals, international universities, and industry partners.
