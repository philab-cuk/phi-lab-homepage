"Data is the new soil, not oil."
— David McCandless

Oil is valuable and tradable. Data only becomes valuable with considerable effort. In the data-driven era, the effectiveness of AI machines depends on the granularity and precision of the data. We focus on optimal informative transformation with the right application, so that it could be a medium that bridges the gap between raw data and actionable knowledge.

Research Focus

Three Core Pillars

Our research is organized around three interconnected domains that span the full lifecycle from raw health data to clinical evidence.

Pillar 01

Knowledge Representation

Data modeling, data engineering, pipeline construction, data management & governance. We build the semantic and structural foundations that make health data queryable, reproducible, and interoperable across systems.

Data Modeling Pipeline Construction Data Governance EHR Structuring
Pillar 02

Real-World Data RWD

Secondary use of real-world data including EHR/CDW data, public big data repositories such as FAERS, HIRA claims, and disease registries. We extract signal from large-scale observational datasets with rigorous methodological standards.

EHR/CDW FAERS HIRA Claims Data Registry
Pillar 03

Real-World Evidence RWE

Scientific data processing, analytics methods for systematic evidence generation. We apply rigorous causal inference, pharmacoepidemiology, and outcomes research methodology to transform observational data into reliable clinical guidance.

Evidence Generation Pharmacovigilance Outcome Research Cohort Studies

Biography

Hyo Jung Kim is an assistant professor in the Department of Biomedical Software Engineering at The Catholic University of Korea, South Korea. She earned her BSc and MSc in Nursing, and Management & Education from Yonsei University in Seoul, South Korea, and her Ph.D. in Biomedical Informatics from Seoul National University. She is a seasoned clinical informaticist with over 20 years of professional experience across clinical practice, EHR software architecture, and academic research.
